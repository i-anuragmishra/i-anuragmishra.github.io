About Me

I’m Anurag Mishra, a dedicated AI researcher and engineer with a Master’s degree in Artificial Intelligence from RIT. My journey in AI is driven by a deep curiosity to understand and address the complexities of machine learning and its impact on society. With expertise in natural language processing, machine learning, and computer vision, I’ve worked on groundbreaking projects like bias detection in Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) systems and contributed to research that has been accepted at leading conferences, including NeurIPS.

Beyond research, I’ve honed my leadership and organizational skills as the Secretary of the RIT AI Club, where I’ve fostered collaboration, hosted events, and driven discussions around cutting-edge AI topics. My work has also intersected with societal impact, as I’ve collaborated on projects focused on accessibility, such as using AI and GIS to improve emergency services for the deaf and hard-of-hearing community.

I am passionate about building AI systems that are not only innovative but also ethical, fair, and practical. Whether it’s exploring the nuances of bias in AI models or designing robust solutions for complex problems, I thrive on bridging the gap between theoretical research and real-world application. I’m always eager to tackle new challenges, collaborate with diverse teams, and contribute to shaping the future of AI.
